Schengen Visa for Indians

Overview

  • Visa Type: Short-stay Tourist Visa (Type C)
  • Validity: Up to 90 days within 180-day period
  • Countries Covered: 27 Schengen countries (includes Czech Republic, Austria, Germany, Slovakia, Hungary)
  • Apply At: Czech Republic Embassy (since Prague is your main destination)

Application Timeline

StepTiming
Start preparing documents3-4 months before travel
Book visa appointment2-3 months before travel
Submit application6 weeks - 15 days before travel
Processing time15-30 days typically
Important: During peak season (April is moderately busy), appointment slots fill up 1-2 months in advance. Book early!

Required Documents

  1. Application Form - Download from VFS website, fill completely, sign at the end
  2. Passport - Valid for 3+ months after return date, at least 2 blank pages
  3. Photographs - 2 recent passport-size photos, white background, 35mm x 45mm
  4. Travel Insurance - Minimum €30,000 coverage, must cover all Schengen countries
  5. Flight Itinerary - Confirmed bookings OR reservation
  6. Accommodation Proof - Hotel bookings for all nights
  7. Travel Itinerary - Day-by-day plan
  8. Financial Documents - Last 6 months bank statements, recommended balance ₹2+ lakhs
  9. Employment Proof - NOC letter from employer, leave approval letter
  10. Cover Letter - Purpose of visit, travel dates, why returning to India

Pro Tips for Visa Success

  1. Strong ties to India: Emphasize job, property, family
  2. Consistent story: Itinerary should match bookings
  3. Sufficient funds: ₹50,000-60,000/person visible balance
  4. No gaps: Account for every day of the trip
  5. Group application: Apply together, shows genuine group travel

Important Driving Rules

  • Drive on RIGHT side
  • Headlights: ON at all times (mandatory in some countries)
  • Blood alcohol: 0.0% in Czech, Hungary, Slovakia; 0.05% in Austria, Germany
  • Seatbelts: Mandatory for all passengers
  • Mobile phones: Hands-free only

Before Driving Off - CHECK & DOCUMENT:

  1. Take photos/videos of entire car - ALL angles
  2. Note existing scratches on rental agreement
  3. Check spare tire, jack, warning triangle
  4. Ensure fuel tank is full
  5. Test all lights
  6. Get emergency numbers

Safety & Health

General Safety

  • Central Europe is very safe for tourists
  • Petty theft (pickpockets) main concern in tourist areas
  • Keep valuables in front pockets
  • Use hotel safes

High-risk areas for pickpockets:

  • Prague: Charles Bridge, Old Town Square, trams
  • Vienna: Stephansplatz, metro
  • Budapest: Váci utca, metro line 4

Healthcare

  • European hospitals are excellent
  • Carry EHIC or travel insurance card
  • Emergency number: 112 (EU-wide)
  • Keep insurance helpline number handy

Travel Insurance Must Include:

  • Medical emergencies: €30,000+ minimum
  • Medical evacuation/repatriation
  • Trip cancellation
  • Lost/stolen luggage
  • Personal liability
  • COVID coverage (check policy)

Cultural Tips

Czech Republic

  • Greeting: "Dobrý den" (Good day)
  • Thank you: "Děkuji"
  • Beer culture is sacred
  • Tipping: Round up or 10%

Austria

  • Greeting: "Grüß Gott"
  • Thank you: "Danke"
  • Very formal culture
  • Punctuality is important
  • Don't jaywalk!

Hungary

  • Greeting: "Jó napot" (Good day)
  • Thank you: "Köszönöm"
  • Don't clink beer glasses! (historical tradition)
  • Tipping: 10-15% expected

General European Tips

  1. Greet shopkeepers when entering/leaving stores
  2. Say please and thank you in local language
  3. Quiet voices in public transport
  4. Dress smart casual - Europeans dress well
  5. Don't be the loud tourist
