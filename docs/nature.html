Bohemian Switzerland National Park - TOP PICK

Why It's Special

  • Europe's largest natural sandstone arch (Pravčická Brána)
  • Dramatic sandstone formations
  • Dense forests and deep gorges
  • Peaceful boat rides through canyons
  • Way less touristy than other nature spots

Getting There

  • From Prague: 90 minutes by car
  • Parking: Hřensko village (€8/day)
  • Leave Prague by 7 AM for best experience

Must-Do Activities

1. Pravčická Brána Hike

  • The iconic sandstone arch
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 2-3 hours round trip
  • Path is well-marked with switchbacks
  • Amazing viewpoint at the top

2. Kamenice Gorge Boat Ride

  • Peaceful punt boats through narrow canyon
  • Boatmen pole through shallow water
  • Two sections: Edmundova and Divoká
  • Duration: 30-45 minutes total
  • Cost: ~€8 per section

3. Mariina Skála Viewpoint

  • Panoramic forest views
  • Wooden viewing platform
  • Short hike from main paths

4. Tisá Rocks

  • Labyrinth of sandstone formations
  • Narnia filming location!
  • Good for scrambling and photos

Suggested Itinerary

  • 7:00 AM - Leave Prague
  • 8:30 AM - Arrive Hřensko, park car
  • 9:00 AM - Start hike to Pravčická Brána
  • 11:30 AM - Reach arch, explore, photos
  • 12:30 PM - Descend, grab lunch at Falcon's Nest restaurant
  • 2:00 PM - Kamenice Gorge boat rides
  • 3:30 PM - Optional: Explore more trails
  • 5:00 PM - Drive back to Prague

What to Bring

  • Hiking shoes (trails can be slippery)
  • Water (2L per person)
  • Packed lunch or snacks
  • Rain jacket (weather changes fast)
  • Camera!
  • Cash (limited card acceptance)
  • Passport (if crossing into Germany)

Tips

  1. Start early - day trippers arrive by 11 AM
  2. Check weather forecast
  3. Some trails may be closed (landslide risk)
  4. Dogs allowed on leash
  5. Combine with Saxon Switzerland (Germany) if you have time

Cost Estimate

  • Parking: €8
  • Boat rides: €16 (both sections)
  • Food: €10-15
  • Total: ~€35/person

Vltava River Rafting - Český Krumlov

Why Do It

  • Scenic float through fairytale landscape
  • Views of Český Krumlov castle from water
  • Easy, fun, suitable for beginners
  • Optional splash zones

Options

Short Route: Vyšší Brod to Český Krumlov

  • Distance: ~12 km
  • Duration: 2-3 hours
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Best for first-timers

Long Route: Rožmberk to Český Krumlov

  • Distance: ~25 km
  • Duration: 4-5 hours
  • Some rapids
  • More adventurous

What's Included (typical)

  • Raft or canoe rental
  • Paddles and life jackets
  • Watertight barrel for belongings
  • Transfer back to start

Tips

  1. Waterproof bag for phone/camera
  2. Wear clothes you don't mind getting wet
  3. Bring change of clothes in car
  4. Sunscreen even on cloudy days
  5. Don't drink too much beer before!

Cost

  • Raft (4 people): €50-80
  • Canoe (2 people): €30-40
  • Per person estimate: €15-25
Book in advance for April - shoulder season but still popular

Austrian Lake District (Salzkammergut)

The Hallstatt Question

Pros:

  • Instagram-famous for a reason
  • Stunning alpine lake + village
  • Salt mine history

Cons:

  • Overtourism is REAL
  • 10,000 visitors/day in tiny village
  • Local protests against tourism
  • Can feel like Disneyland

Alternatives to Hallstatt

St. Wolfgang - RECOMMENDED

  • Just as beautiful
  • Much fewer tourists
  • Lake Wolfgangsee
  • Schafbergbahn railway to summit
  • More authentic feel

Bad Ischl

  • Former imperial summer retreat
  • Spa town vibes
  • Great base for exploring region

Königssee (Germany)

  • Even more dramatic than Hallstatt
  • Electric boat tours
  • St. Bartholomä church on lake
  • About 1 hour from Salzburg

If You Do Visit Hallstatt

Timing is Everything:

  • Before 9 AM: Peaceful, magical
  • 10 AM - 4 PM: Overcrowded chaos
  • After 5 PM: Crowds thin, beautiful evening light

Best Approach:

  • Stay overnight (support local economy)
  • Morning walk before crowds
  • Leave by noon
  • Continue to Salzburg

Activities in the Region

  • Boat Tours - Lake Hallstatt, Lake Wolfgangsee
  • Salt Mine Tour (Hallstatt) - 7,000 years of history, €40/person
  • Dachstein Ice Caves - Frozen waterfalls inside mountain

Thermal Baths - Budapest - UNIQUE EXPERIENCE

Why Budapest for Thermal Baths

  • City built on 120+ hot springs
  • Bathing culture since Roman times
  • Stunning historic bath houses
  • Perfect post-hiking recovery

Top Bath Houses

Szechenyi Thermal Bath - MOST RECOMMENDED

  • Largest medicinal bath in Europe
  • Iconic yellow neo-baroque building
  • 18 pools (indoor + outdoor)
  • Famous chess-playing pool
  • Open year-round
  • Entry: €25-30
  • Hours: 6 AM - 10 PM

Gellért Thermal Bath

  • Art Nouveau masterpiece
  • Attached to Gellért Hotel
  • Wave pool!
  • More upscale feel
  • Entry: €25-30

Rudas Bath

  • Ottoman-era (500 years old!)
  • Night bathing on weekends
  • Rooftop pool with views
  • Mixed days vs. single-gender days
  • Entry: €20-25

Király Bath

  • Smallest, most authentic Ottoman bath
  • Dome with star-shaped skylights
  • Very local experience
  • Entry: €20

What to Bring

  • Swimsuit (mandatory)
  • Flip-flops (for walking around)
  • Towel (can rent for €3-4)
  • Waterproof bag for valuables
  • Small padlock (lockers available)

Tips

  1. Book Széchenyi online - skip ticket lines
  2. Arrive early (before 9 AM) for peaceful experience
  3. Don't eat heavy meal before
  4. Stay 2-3 hours minimum
  5. Hydrate! Hot water is dehydrating
  6. Shower before entering pools (required)
  7. Follow posted pool temperatures
  8. Great for hangovers after Budapest nightlife!

Bath Etiquette

  • Shower before entering any pool
  • No diving or splashing
  • Keep voices down
  • No photos in changing areas
  • Respect personal space
  • Swimming caps required in some pools

Sparty (Saturday Night Pool Party)

  • Széchenyi transforms into club
  • DJs, lasers, dancing in pools
  • €50-70 includes open bar
  • Unique experience!

Day Trip: Dresden + Saxon Switzerland

Double Feature Day

Morning: Dresden (2-3 hours)

  • Zwinger Palace
  • Frauenkirche
  • Old Masters Gallery

Afternoon: Bastei Bridge

  • Just 30 minutes from Dresden
  • Iconic sandstone bridge
  • Dramatic viewpoints
  • Easy walks around area

Why It Works

  • Dresden is en route between Prague and Saxon Switzerland
  • Get culture AND nature in one day
  • Only 2 hours from Prague

Best Scenic Driving Routes

1. Prague → Bohemian Switzerland

  • Through rolling Bohemian countryside
  • Small villages
  • Stop at viewpoints

2. Salzburg → Hallstatt

  • Alpine scenery
  • Lake views
  • Mountain passes

3. Salzkammergut Lakes Loop

  • Multiple lake viewpoints
  • Charming villages
  • Perfect for photo stops
