Public Transport Itinerary No Car Needed

15-Day Central Europe Trip | April 11-25, 2026 | Trains, Buses & Shuttles

Why Public Transport?

Based on extensive research from Reddit, Rick Steves forums, and TripAdvisor, dropping the rental cars for this route makes a lot of sense:

  • No parking hassles in Prague, Vienna, Budapest (expensive + difficult)
  • No cross-border fees (can be 100-300+ per car)
  • No driving stress - European roads, signs, rules
  • Drink freely on party nights without worrying about driving next day
  • Trains are often faster than driving between major cities
  • More relaxing - enjoy scenery, nap, chat with friends
Estimated Savings: 5,000-15,000 per person
(No car rental, insurance, cross-border fees, fuel, vignettes, parking)
The Route:
Prague (Start) --> Cesky Krumlov --> Salzburg --> Innsbruck --> Vienna --> Budapest --> Prague (End)
"For this itinerary, there is no need to rent a car. It is very easy to travel between Budapest, Vienna, and Prague by train." - TripAdvisor community consensus

Key Route Modification

The main change from the car version: Hallstatt becomes a day trip from Salzburg rather than an overnight stay. This is actually how most travelers do it by public transport, and locals recommend it because:

  • Hallstatt is tiny - you only need 3-4 hours there
  • Accommodation in Hallstatt is expensive and limited
  • The train+ferry journey is scenic and part of the experience
  • More time in Salzburg (which has more to offer)

Day-by-Day Itinerary

DAY 1 - Friday, April 11: Arrival in Prague

Fly into: Prague (PRG) - Vaclav Havel Airport

Airport Transfer: Airport Express Bus (AE) to main train station - 30 min, ~100 CZK (~400). Or Uber/Bolt ~500.

Activities:

  • Arrive and take airport bus/taxi to city center
  • Check into accommodation in Old Town or Vinohrady
  • Evening walk through Old Town Square
  • Welcome dinner at a traditional Czech restaurant
  • Light drinks - save energy for tomorrow!

Accommodation: Prague (~1,500-2,500/person/night)

DAY 2 - Saturday, April 12: Prague City + Party Night 1

Type: Historical + Party

Morning:

  • Prague Castle complex (3-4 hours)
  • St. Vitus Cathedral
  • Golden Lane

Afternoon:

  • Walk across Charles Bridge
  • Lesser Town (Mala Strana) exploration
  • Petrin Hill for views (take funicular - included in transit pass)
PARTY NIGHT 1: Karlovy Lazne (5-floor megaclub) or Cross Club (industrial techno). Beer is cheaper than water - enjoy! No driving tomorrow until afternoon.

Transport: Prague day pass 120 CZK (~500) covers all trams, metro, funicular

DAY 3 - Sunday, April 13: Prague --> Cesky Krumlov Bus 2h 35m

Type: Fairytale Town + Chill Day

Transport: RegioJet or FlixBus from Prague Na Knizeci to Cesky Krumlov Spicak. Buses every hour. Book at regiojet.com or flixbus.com. Cost: 6-14 per person.

Morning:

  • Sleep in, recover from last night
  • Brunch in Prague
  • Take 12:00 or 13:00 bus (no need to rush!)

Afternoon:

  • Arrive Cesky Krumlov around 3 PM
  • Bus station is 5-minute walk to Old Town
  • Check in, explore the medieval streets

Evening:

  • Cesky Krumlov Castle viewpoint at sunset
  • Dinner at a riverside restaurant
  • Relaxed drinks - enjoy the fairytale atmosphere

Accommodation: Cesky Krumlov (~1,200-2,000/person)

Day-trippers leave by 5 PM. The town transforms into a quiet, magical place. This is why you stay overnight!

DAY 4 - Monday, April 14: Cesky Krumlov --> Salzburg Shuttle 3h

Type: Scenic Transfer + Mozart City

Transport: CK Shuttle or Bean Shuttle - door-to-door service in 8-seat minivan. They pick you up from your hotel! Book at ckshuttle.cz or beanshuttle.com. Cost: 25-35 per person.

Morning:

  • Early morning walk through Krumlov (best light for photos)
  • Breakfast at a local cafe
  • Shuttle picks you up at 10:00 or 11:00 (book specific time)

Afternoon:

  • Scenic drive through Austrian countryside
  • Arrive Salzburg around 1-2 PM
  • Shuttle drops you at your hotel

Evening:

  • Explore Altstadt (Old Town) - very walkable!
  • Mirabell Gardens (free, Sound of Music location!)
  • Dinner with views of Hohensalzburg Fortress
  • Drinks on Rudolfskai bar street

Accommodation: Salzburg (~2,000-3,000/person) - stay 2 nights

Tip: Book the shuttle 1-2 weeks ahead in April. They fill up!

DAY 5 - Tuesday, April 15: Hallstatt Day Trip Train + Ferry

Type: Alpine Lake Village

Transport: Bus 150 from Salzburg Hbf to Bad Ischl (1h 15m), then train to Hallstatt Bahnhof (25min), then ferry across the lake (7min). Total: ~2.5 hours, 14.60 each way. Or take organized tour (~50).
Why Day Trip Works Better: Hallstatt is tiny (800 residents). 3-4 hours is plenty. Crowds are 10 AM - 5 PM, so arrive early, leave by 4 PM. No need to pay premium for overnight stay.

Early Morning:

  • Take 8:45 bus from Salzburg Hbf
  • Arrive Hallstatt around 11:00
  • The ferry ride across the lake is magical!

Midday:

  • Famous lakeside viewpoint photo
  • Wander the tiny streets
  • Lunch at a lakeside restaurant
  • Optional: Salt mine tour or Skywalk (add 2 hours)

Afternoon:

  • Take 3:30 or 4:30 PM ferry back
  • Return to Salzburg by 6-7 PM

Evening:

  • Dinner in Salzburg
  • Relaxed evening - big train day tomorrow
"You don't need a car in Hallstatt. It's tiny and all pedestrian. The train + ferry is part of the experience!" - Rick Steves Forum

DAY 6 - Wednesday, April 16: Salzburg --> Innsbruck Train 1h 50m

Type: Transfer + Alpine City

Transport: OBB Railjet from Salzburg Hbf to Innsbruck Hbf. Trains every hour. Beautiful alpine scenery! Book at oebb.at. Cost: 20-35.

Morning:

  • Visit Hohensalzburg Fortress (funicular up)
  • Mozart's Birthplace photo op
  • Grab coffee and Sachertorte

Midday:

  • Take 12:00 or 13:00 train to Innsbruck
  • Enjoy the alpine scenery from the train!
  • Arrive Innsbruck around 2 PM

Afternoon:

  • Check in to accommodation (central = walking distance to everything)
  • Walk through Maria-Theresien-Strasse
  • Golden Roof photo
  • Explore the old town

Evening:

  • Dinner in the old town
  • Early-ish night - big mountain day tomorrow!

Accommodation: Innsbruck (~2,500-3,500/person)

DAY 7 - Thursday, April 17: Innsbruck Full Day - Nordkette

Type: Alpine Adventure

Nordkette Mountain: Cable car takes you from city center to 2,334m in 20 minutes. Views of the entire Tyrolean Alps! No car needed - the Hungerburgbahn station is right in the old town.

Morning:

  • Nordkette cable car (Hungerburgbahn --> Seegrube --> Hafelekar)
  • Buy combined ticket (~47)
  • Spend 2-3 hours at the top
  • Short hikes available at summit

Afternoon:

  • Descend and grab lunch
  • Explore old town - Hofburg Palace, Hofkirche
  • Coffee and cake at a traditional cafe

Evening:

  • Dinner at a traditional Tyrolean restaurant
  • Pack for Vienna tomorrow
April at 2,300m can be cold (-2 to 5C). Bring warm layers! The views are worth it.

DAY 8 - Friday, April 18: Innsbruck --> Vienna Train 4h 15m

Type: Scenic Train + Imperial City

Transport: OBB Railjet direct from Innsbruck to Vienna Hbf. Beautiful alpine-to-plains scenery! Book at oebb.at. Cost: 30-50. Book early for best prices.

Morning:

  • Last walk through Innsbruck
  • Breakfast at a cafe
  • Take 9:00 or 10:00 train

Afternoon:

  • Arrive Vienna around 2-3 PM
  • Check into accommodation in Innere Stadt or Neubau
  • Vienna has excellent U-bahn (metro) - get a 48-hour pass (14.10)

Evening:

  • Evening walk along the Ringstrasse
  • Dinner at Naschmarkt area (if staying nearby)
  • Rest up - party night tomorrow!

Accommodation: Vienna (~2,000-3,000/person) - stay 2 nights

DAY 9 - Saturday, April 19: Vienna Full Day + Party Night 2

Type: Culture + Nightlife

Morning:

  • Schonbrunn Palace (U4 metro direct, arrive early, book online!)
  • Palace gardens are free and gorgeous

Afternoon:

  • Belvedere Palace (Klimt's "The Kiss")
  • St. Stephen's Cathedral
  • Coffee and cake at Cafe Central or Cafe Sacher
PARTY NIGHT 2: Vienna nightlife is underrated! Try Pratersauna (club in a former sauna by the Danube), Flex (legendary electronic music), or bar-hop in the 7th District. No driving tomorrow until afternoon!

Transport: Vienna 48-hour pass covers unlimited U-bahn, trams, buses

DAY 10 - Sunday, April 20: Vienna --> Budapest Train 2h 30m

Type: Easy Train + Arrive Budapest

Transport: OBB Railjet from Vienna Hbf to Budapest Keleti. Trains every 1-2 hours. Very comfortable! Can stop in Bratislava if you want (it's on the way). Cost: 16-30.

Morning:

  • Sleep in, recover from last night
  • Brunch somewhere nice in Vienna
  • Take 13:00 or 14:00 train

Optional Bratislava Stop:

  • Stop for 2-3 hours - it's on the train route!
  • Vienna --> Bratislava: 1 hour
  • Bratislava --> Budapest: 2.5 hours
  • Quick castle visit + old town walk + lunch

Evening:

  • Arrive Budapest by 5-6 PM
  • Check into District VII (Jewish Quarter)
  • Light dinner, rest up for the next two days!

Accommodation: Budapest District VII (~1,500-2,500/person) - stay 3 nights

Pro Tip: District VII is walking distance to everything + the ruin bars. You won't need public transport much here!

DAY 11 - Monday, April 21: Budapest Day 1 + Party Night 3

Type: Thermal Baths + History + Ruin Bars

Morning:

  • Szechenyi Thermal Baths - Iconic yellow bath complex
  • Take M1 metro to Szechenyi furdo station
  • Soak in outdoor pools, play chess in the water

Afternoon:

  • Heroes' Square (right next to the baths)
  • Walk down Andrassy Avenue
  • Hungarian Parliament Building (book tour in advance)
  • Shoes on the Danube memorial
PARTY NIGHT 3: Ruin bar intro! Start at Szimpla Kert (6 PM), then Mazel Tov, then Instant-Fogas. These are legendary - free entry, 2-4 drinks, party until sunrise! All walkable from District VII.

Transport: Budapest 24-hour pass ~2,500 HUF (~600). But District VII is mostly walkable.

DAY 12 - Tuesday, April 22: Budapest Day 2 + Party Night 4

Type: Explore + Epic Finale

Late Morning:

  • Sleep in!
  • Brunch at a District VII cafe

Afternoon:

  • Walk across Chain Bridge to Buda side
  • Take funicular up to Buda Castle (or walk)
  • Fisherman's Bastion for views
  • Great street art in the Jewish Quarter
PARTY NIGHT 4 - THE FINALE: Full ruin bar crawl! Eleszto (craft beer) --> Koleves Kert (hammocks) --> Instant-Fogas (multi-room club until 6 AM). Consider booking SPARTY pool party if it's on!
This is your last big party night. Make it count! Tomorrow is a chill travel day.

DAY 13 - Wednesday, April 23: Budapest --> Prague Train 7h

Type: Long Train + Recovery

Transport: RegioJet train from Budapest Keleti to Prague hl.n. 7 hours but very comfortable! Free WiFi, coffee, at-seat service. Or take night train and save a hotel night. Book at regiojet.com. Cost: 16-25.

Morning:

  • Sleep in as late as possible
  • Pack up, late brunch
  • Take 11:00 or 12:00 train

Afternoon:

  • 7-hour train to Prague - perfect recovery!
  • Sleep, watch movies, read, chat
  • RegioJet has free coffee, water, snacks
  • This IS your rest day - embrace it

Evening:

  • Arrive Prague around 6-7 PM
  • Check into final accommodation
  • Chill dinner, early night

Accommodation: Prague (~1,500-2,500/person)

Alternative: Take the EuroNight sleeper train (departs ~8 PM, arrives ~6 AM). Save a hotel night + wake up in Prague! Couchette ~40.

DAY 14 - Thursday, April 24: Buffer Day - Choose Your Adventure

This day is intentionally flexible. Choose based on how you're feeling:

Option A: Bohemian Switzerland (Recommended!)

Best Option: Organized tour from Prague. Includes transport, guide, Pravcicka Gate, boat ride, lunch. ~100/person. Book via Bohemia Adventures, Northern Hikes, or Viator.
  • Pickup from Prague around 8 AM
  • Europe's largest natural sandstone arch
  • Kamenice Gorge boat ride
  • Saxon Switzerland (Germany) viewpoints
  • Return to Prague by 6-7 PM

Note: Public transport to Bohemian Switzerland is possible but takes 3 hours each way. The tour is worth it!

Option B: Kutna Hora (half day, less effort)

Transport: Direct train from Prague hl.n. to Kutna Hora - 1 hour, ~150 CZK return.
  • Sedlec Ossuary (Bone Church) - incredibly unique
  • St. Barbara's Cathedral
  • Back in Prague by 2 PM

Option C: Rest Day in Prague (if you're exhausted)

  • Sleep in late
  • Letna Park beer garden with city views
  • Explore Vinohrady neighborhood
  • Final souvenir shopping

Evening (all options):

  • Farewell dinner at a nice restaurant
  • Final drinks in Prague's Old Town
  • Pack for departure

DAY 15 - Friday, April 25: Departure

Type: Travel Day

To Airport: Airport Express bus from Prague main station - 35 min, ~100 CZK. Or Uber/Bolt ~500.

Morning:

  • Check out of accommodation
  • Last walk through Old Town
  • Last coffee and trdelnik

Afternoon/Evening:

  • Head to Prague Airport
  • Fly back to India (typically evening flight)
  • Land in India: Saturday, April 26

The Bottom Line

Dropping the cars saves money, reduces stress, and lets you fully enjoy the nightlife. The trains between cities are comfortable and scenic. The only real trade-off is Bohemian Switzerland - but the organized tour is actually recommended by locals because guides know the trails and it includes the boat ride + lunch.

For a group focused on cities + nightlife + some nature, public transport is the smarter choice.
