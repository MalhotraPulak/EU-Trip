# Central Europe Road Trip - April 2026

## Trip Overview
- **Duration:** 15 Days
- **Travelers:** 5 people (2 girls, 3 guys, ages 25-27)
- **Drivers:** 3 people available
- **Vehicles:** 2 rental cars
- **Budget:** ₹2-2.5 Lakh per person
- **Theme:** Nature + History + Nightlife

---

## Recommended Route: The Central Europe Loop

### Route Map
```
Prague (Start) → Bohemian Switzerland → Dresden → Prague → Český Krumlov →
Hallstatt/Salzburg → Vienna → Bratislava → Budapest → (Return to Prague)
```

**Total Driving Distance:** ~1,400 km
**Countries Covered:** Czech Republic, Germany, Austria, Slovakia, Hungary

---

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### DAY 1: Arrival in Prague
**Fly into:** Prague (PRG) - Václav Havel Airport

**Activities:**
- Arrive and pick up rental cars from airport (Sixt/Hertz recommended)
- Check into accommodation in Old Town or Vinohrady district
- Evening walk through Old Town Square
- Welcome dinner at a traditional Czech restaurant
- Night out in Prague's bar scene (warm-up before the big nights!)

**Accommodation:** Hostel or Airbnb in Prague (~₹1,500-2,500/person/night)

**Must See:**
- Astronomical Clock (every hour on the hour)
- Old Town Square at night

---

### DAY 2: Prague City Day
**Type:** Historical + Party

**Morning:**
- Prague Castle complex (allow 3-4 hours)
- St. Vitus Cathedral
- Golden Lane

**Afternoon:**
- Walk across Charles Bridge
- Lesser Town (Malá Strana) exploration
- Petřín Hill for views (take funicular)

**Evening:**
- Dinner in Old Town
- **PARTY NIGHT:** Prague has Europe's largest nightclub - Karlovy Lázně (5 floors!)
- Alternative: Cross Club (industrial techno vibes)
- Beer is cheaper than water in Prague - enjoy!

**Budget Tip:** Beer costs €1-2 in local pubs, €3-4 in tourist areas

---

### DAY 3: Bohemian Switzerland National Park
**Type:** Nature Day
**Driving:** ~2 hours from Prague

**Split into 2 cars and head north!**

**Must Do:**
- **Pravčická Brána** - Europe's largest natural sandstone arch
- **Kamenice Gorge** - Peaceful boat ride through the canyon
- **Bastei Bridge** (if crossing into Saxon Switzerland, Germany)

**Hiking:**
- Trail to Pravčická Gate is beginner-friendly (~1 hour)
- Bring proper hiking shoes

**Tips:**
- Start early (8 AM) to avoid crowds
- Pack lunch - limited food options in the park
- Parking at Hřensko village (~€8/day)
- Bring passports if crossing into German Saxon Switzerland

**Return to Prague:** Evening
**Dinner:** Grab street food at a Prague food market

---

### DAY 4: Prague → Český Krumlov
**Driving:** ~2.5 hours
**Type:** Fairytale Town + Chill Day

**Why Stay Overnight:**
- Day-trippers leave by 5 PM
- The town transforms into a magical, quiet fairytale
- Best photos at sunrise/sunset without crowds

**Activities:**
- Český Krumlov Castle (second largest in Czech Republic)
- Rafting on the Vltava River (book in advance!)
- Wander the cobblestone streets
- Watch the sunset from the castle viewpoint

**Evening:**
- Dinner at The House of Two Maries (traditional Bohemian)
- Relaxed drinks at a riverside bar

**Accommodation:** Hostel Postel or Penzion (₹1,200-2,000/person)

---

### DAY 5: Český Krumlov → Hallstatt Region → Salzburg
**Driving:** ~3.5 hours
**Type:** Scenic Drive + Nature

**Important Note on Hallstatt:**
Hallstatt is Instagram-famous but extremely overcrowded (10,000 tourists/day in a town of 800). Consider these alternatives:
- **St. Wolfgang** - Similar beauty, fewer tourists
- **Sankt Gilgen** - Picturesque lakeside town
- **Bad Ischl** - Former imperial summer retreat

**If You Do Visit Hallstatt:**
- Arrive before 9 AM or after 4 PM
- Don't plan more than 2-3 hours
- The famous viewpoint gets very crowded

**Evening in Salzburg:**
- Explore Altstadt (Old Town)
- Dinner with views of Hohensalzburg Fortress
- Salzburg has a great bar scene on Rudolfskai

**Accommodation:** Salzburg hostel/Airbnb (₹2,000-3,000/person)

---

### DAY 6: Salzburg → Vienna
**Driving:** ~3 hours
**Type:** Historical + Cultural

**Morning in Salzburg:**
- Mirabell Gardens (free, Sound of Music location!)
- Quick visit to Mozart's birthplace
- Grab a coffee and Sachertorte

**Afternoon: Drive to Vienna**
- Scenic route through Austrian countryside
- Stop at Melk Abbey if time permits (stunning Baroque monastery)

**Evening in Vienna:**
- Check into accommodation near Innere Stadt (1st District)
- Evening walk along the Ringstrasse
- Dinner at Naschmarkt area (if open) or local Beisl

**Accommodation:** Wombat's City Hostel or 25hours Hotel at MuseumsQuartier

---

### DAY 7: Vienna Full Day
**Type:** Culture + Nightlife

**Morning:**
- Schönbrunn Palace (arrive early, book online!)
- Palace gardens are free and gorgeous

**Afternoon:**
- Belvedere Palace (Klimt's "The Kiss" is here)
- St. Stephen's Cathedral
- Coffee and cake at Café Central or Café Sacher

**Evening:**
- Dinner in the 7th District (Neubau) - trendy restaurants
- Vienna's nightlife is underrated - try:
  - **Pratersauna** - Club in a former sauna by the Danube
  - **Flex** - Legendary electronic music club
  - **Elektro Gönner** - Quirky bar in an electric shop

**Tips:**
- Vienna is expensive - budget €60-80/person for the day
- Use public transport (24hr pass = €8)

---

### DAY 8: Vienna → Bratislava → Budapest
**Driving:** Vienna to Bratislava (1 hour), Bratislava to Budapest (2.5 hours)
**Type:** Two Capitals in One Day

**Morning in Vienna:**
- Sleep in, recover from last night
- Brunch somewhere nice

**Bratislava Stop (3-4 hours):**
- Very underrated capital city!
- Bratislava Castle for views
- Old Town walking tour (very compact)
- Lunch - much cheaper than Vienna!
- Slovakia uses EURO (convenient!)

**Evening: Arrive in Budapest**
- Check into accommodation in District VII (Jewish Quarter)
- This is where all the ruin bars are!
- Light dinner, rest up for tomorrow

**Accommodation:** Wombat's Budapest or local hostel in District VII (₹1,500-2,500/person)

---

### DAY 9: Budapest Day 1
**Type:** Thermal Baths + History

**Morning:**
- **Széchenyi Thermal Baths** - Iconic yellow bath complex
- Soak in outdoor pools, play chess in the water
- Book "Sparty" for Saturday night (pool party!)

**Afternoon:**
- Heroes' Square
- Walk down Andrássy Avenue (like Budapest's Champs-Élysées)
- Hungarian Parliament Building (book tour in advance)
- Shoes on the Danube (Holocaust memorial) - very moving

**Evening:**
- Dinner at a traditional Hungarian restaurant (try goulash!)
- Sunset from Fisherman's Bastion
- Cross the Chain Bridge at night (beautifully lit)

---

### DAY 10: Budapest Day 2 - PARTY DAY
**Type:** Ruin Bars + Nightlife

**Late Morning/Afternoon:**
- Sleep in!
- Brunch at a District VII café
- Explore the Jewish Quarter during daylight
- Great Street Art scene

**Evening - The Main Event: Ruin Bars!**
Budapest's ruin bars are legendary - abandoned buildings transformed into eclectic bars.

**Recommended Route:**
1. **Szimpla Kert** (6 PM) - The original, start here for dinner/drinks
2. **Mazel Tov** - Great food + courtyard vibes
3. **Instant-Fogas Complex** - Multi-room club, open until 6 AM
4. **Élesztő** - Craft beer heaven
5. **Kőleves Kert** - Local favorite with hammocks

**Pro Tips:**
- Ruin bars are FREE entry
- Drinks are CHEAP (€2-4 for beers)
- Most open around 4-6 PM
- Peak time is after midnight
- Dress casual - there's no dress code
- Budapest nightlife goes until sunrise!

---

### DAY 11: Budapest Recovery + Departure
**Type:** Chill Day
**Driving:** Budapest → Prague (~5 hours direct, or break it up)

**Morning:**
- Sleep in, pack up
- Grab a late brunch
- Optional: Quick visit to Buda Castle if you missed it

**Afternoon:**
- Begin drive back toward Prague
- **Option A:** Drive direct to Prague (arrive evening)
- **Option B:** Stop in Brno, Czech Republic (halfway point, nice university city)

**If stopping in Brno:**
- Walk around the old town
- Grab dinner at a local pub
- Continue to Prague next morning

---

### DAY 12: Flexible Day - Choose Your Adventure

**Option A: Dresden, Germany (Recommended)**
- Drive from Prague: 2 hours
- Stunning Baroque city rebuilt after WWII
- Old Masters Gallery (Raphael, Vermeer, Rubens)
- Frauenkirche (iconic rebuilt church)
- Green Vault (royal treasury)
- Great Christmas markets if visiting in December!

**Option B: Kutná Hora**
- Drive from Prague: 1 hour
- Famous Sedlec Ossuary (Bone Church) - incredibly unique
- UNESCO World Heritage Gothic cathedral
- Less touristy than other day trips

**Option C: Rest Day in Prague**
- Explore neighborhoods you missed
- Letná Park for beer garden + city views
- Vyšehrad fortress (less crowded than Prague Castle)

---

### DAY 13: Prague - Final Exploration
**Type:** Leisure + Shopping

**Activities:**
- Visit any attractions you missed
- Shopping for souvenirs (Bohemian crystal, marionettes)
- Explore Vinohrady or Žižkov neighborhoods (local feel)
- Beer tasting at a Czech microbrewery

**Evening:**
- Fancy farewell dinner
- One last night out in Prague!

---

### DAY 14: Prague - Last Day
**Type:** Chill + Pack

**Morning:**
- Sleep in
- Check out of accommodation
- Return rental cars (check for fuel requirements!)

**Afternoon:**
- Last-minute sightseeing
- Grab lunch near the airport

**Evening:**
- Fly back to India

## Alternative Routes (If You Have More Time)

### Add Slovenia (+3-4 days)
**Route:** Vienna → Ljubljana (3.5 hrs) → Lake Bled → Back to Budapest

**Highlights:**
- Ljubljana - Charming capital, great food
- Lake Bled - Iconic island church, incredible hiking
- Vintgar Gorge - Turquoise waters, easy hike
- Lake Bohinj - Less crowded than Bled

### Add Krakow, Poland (+2-3 days)
**From Budapest:** 5-hour drive north

**Highlights:**
- Auschwitz-Birkenau (sobering but important)
- Wieliczka Salt Mine
- Beautiful Old Town
- Even cheaper than Budapest!

## Important Notes

1. **Cross-Border Driving:** Inform car rental about all countries you'll visit
2. **Vignettes Required:** Austria, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary
3. **April Weather:** 10-18°C, pack layers, rain jacket essential
4. **Book in Advance:** Schönbrunn Palace, thermal baths, rafting
5. **Keep Passports Handy:** Even within Schengen, carry ID
