## Schengen Visa for Indians

### Overview
- **Visa Type:** Short-stay Tourist Visa (Type C)
- **Validity:** Up to 90 days within 180-day period
- **Countries Covered:** 27 Schengen countries (includes Czech Republic, Austria, Germany, Slovakia, Hungary)
- **Apply At:** Czech Republic Embassy (since Prague is your main destination)

### Application Timeline
| Step | Timing |
|------|--------|
| Start preparing documents | 3-4 months before travel |
| Book visa appointment | 2-3 months before travel |
| Submit application | 6 weeks - 15 days before travel |
| Processing time | 15-30 days typically |

**Important:** During peak season (April is moderately busy), appointment slots fill up 1-2 months in advance. Book early!

### Required Documents

**1. Application Form**
- Download from VFS website
- Fill completely, sign at the end

**2. Passport**
- Valid for 3+ months after return date
- At least 2 blank pages
- Include old passports if any

**3. Photographs**
- 2 recent passport-size photos
- White background, 35mm x 45mm
- Not older than 6 months

**4. Travel Insurance**
- Minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 coverage
- Must cover all Schengen countries
- Include medical emergencies, repatriation
- Providers: ICICI Lombard, HDFC Ergo, Tata AIG

**5. Flight Itinerary**
- Confirmed bookings OR reservation
- Tip: Use services like "Dummy Ticket" if not ready to book

**6. Accommodation Proof**
- Hotel bookings for all nights
- Airbnb confirmations work
- Book refundable options initially

**7. Travel Itinerary**
- Day-by-day plan (use our itinerary!)
- Include transportation between cities

**8. Financial Documents**
- Last 6 months bank statements
- **Recommended balance: ‚Çπ2+ lakhs**
- Salary slips (last 3 months)
- ITR for last 2 years
- Forex card/credit card copies

**9. Employment Proof**
- NOC letter from employer
- Leave approval letter
- Company registration documents
- Business card

**10. Cover Letter**
- Purpose of visit
- Travel dates and itinerary
- Why returning to India (job, family, property)

### Visa Fees
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Visa Fee | ‚Ç¨80 (~‚Çπ7,200) |

### Pro Tips for Visa Success
1. **Strong ties to India:** Emphasize job, property, family
2. **Consistent story:** Itinerary should match bookings
3. **Sufficient funds:** ‚Çπ50,000-60,000/person visible balance
4. **No gaps:** Account for every day of the trip
5. **Group application:** Apply together, shows genuine group travel

### VFS Global Process
1. Create account on VFS Czech Republic website
2. Book appointment (earliest available)
3. Submit biometrics and documents
4. Track application online
5. Collect passport (courier or in-person)

### Booking Tips

3. **Book early:** 2-3 months ahead for best rates
4. **Read fine print:** Cross-border fees, mileage limits

### Driving Rules

**Speed Limits:**
| Road Type | Czech | Austria | Germany | Slovakia | Hungary |
|-----------|-------|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| City | 50 | 50 | 50 | 50 | 50 |
| Rural | 90 | 100 | 100 | 90 | 90 |
| Highway | 130 | 130 | None* | 130 | 130 |

*Germany Autobahn: Recommended 130, no limit in some sections

**Important Rules:**
- **Drive on RIGHT side**
- Headlights: ON at all times (mandatory in some countries)
- Blood alcohol: 0.0% in Czech, Hungary, Slovakia; 0.05% in Austria, Germany
- Seatbelts: Mandatory for all passengers
- Mobile phones: Hands-free only
- Winter tires: May not be required in April, but check conditions

**Speed cameras:** VERY common in Europe. Tickets are mailed to rental company and charged to your card.

### Parking

**City Center Parking:**
- Most old towns are pedestrian-only
- Use P+R (Park and Ride) outside center
- Public transport into center

**Costs:**
- Prague: ‚Ç¨2-4/hour in center
- Vienna: ‚Ç¨2-5/hour
- Budapest: ‚Ç¨1-3/hour

**Tip:** Many hotels/Airbnbs don't include parking. Ask in advance!

### Before Driving Off

**CHECK & DOCUMENT:**
1. Take photos/videos of entire car - ALL angles
2. Note existing scratches on rental agreement
3. Check spare tire, jack, warning triangle
4. Ensure fuel tank is full
5. Test all lights
6. Get emergency numbers

### If Something Goes Wrong

**Accident:**
1. Stay calm, check for injuries
2. Call police if serious (EU emergency: 112)
3. Exchange info with other party
4. Take photos of everything
5. Call rental company
6. Get police report

**Breakdown:**
1. Pull over safely, hazard lights on
2. Place warning triangle 50m behind car
3. Call rental company's roadside assistance
4. Don't attempt repairs yourself

## Safety & Health

### General Safety
- Central Europe is very safe for tourists
- Petty theft (pickpockets) main concern in tourist areas
- Keep valuables in front pockets
- Use hotel safes
- Don't flash expensive items

**High-risk areas for pickpockets:**
- Prague: Charles Bridge, Old Town Square, trams
- Vienna: Stephansplatz, metro
- Budapest: V√°ci utca, metro line 4

### Health Precautions

**Before Travel:**
- No special vaccinations required
- Ensure routine vaccines are up to date
- Carry prescription medications in original packaging
- Get dental checkup before travel

**During Travel:**
- Stay hydrated (carry water bottle)
- Don't overdo alcohol (especially at altitude)
- Use sunscreen even in April
- Watch your step on cobblestones!

**Healthcare:**
- European hospitals are excellent
- Carry EHIC or travel insurance card
- Emergency number: 112 (EU-wide)
- Keep insurance helpline number handy

### Travel Insurance Coverage

**Must Include:**
- Medical emergencies: ‚Ç¨30,000+ minimum
- Medical evacuation/repatriation
- Trip cancellation
- Lost/stolen luggage
- Personal liability
- COVID coverage (check policy)

**Recommended Providers:**
- ICICI Lombard
- HDFC Ergo
- Tata AIG
- Bajaj Allianz
